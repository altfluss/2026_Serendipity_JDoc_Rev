%%%%% Section 6. Demonstration with real data %%%%%
\section{Demonstration}\label{sec:demonstration}

Section~\ref{sec:computational} gave the bundle its learning algorithm.
This section is a demonstration: the bundle is learned from real data,
and the resulting representation is offered to the reader as an
explorable system. What we demonstrate is realizability---the concept
takes concrete form from real data.


%%% Subsection 6.1 The data space the bundle requires %%%
\subsection{The data space the bundle requires}\label{subsec:unfolded}

% --- Para 1: 書き出し + 2要件を予告 ---
No algorithm digests raw data directly; some preparation of the data
space is always required. The space $\mathcal{X}$ on which OT-FB
operates must meet two requirements.

% --- Para 2: 要件(1) 局所ユークリッド性 ---
The first comes directly from Eq.~\eqref{eq:objective}. Its cost is the
squared Euclidean distance $\|\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}'\|^2$, so
$\mathcal{X}$ must be a space in which Euclidean distance is locally
meaningful: locally smooth, with nearby points genuinely related.
Nothing is asked of global flatness or large-scale Euclidean
structure---only that, in a neighborhood of each point, Euclidean
distance can be trusted.

% --- Para 3: 要件(2) 局所距離が genre-ness + atmosphere を反映 ---
The second concerns what that local distance measures. It must reflect
affective character: two items affectively similar---genre-ness \emph{and}
atmosphere---must lie near one another in $\mathcal{X}$, and small
displacements must register both. The two layers---genre-ness and
atmosphere---remain entangled in $\mathcal{X}$, yet the geometry holds them
in a form \emph{local} Euclidean distance can read. We call such a space
\emph{unfolded}~\cite{DiCarlo2007333}.

% --- Para 4+5: Unfolding ≠ Disentanglement + raw vs deep ---
Unfolding is not disentangling: the former is a property of $\mathcal{X}$,
the latter of the representation $\Phi$ over it. In raw form, acoustic
features, word counts, and pixel intensities place semantically kindred items
far apart even locally, on an extremely folded manifold~\cite{DiCarlo2007333}---failing both
requirements. Modern deep embeddings satisfy them by
design~\cite{Bengio20131798}; OT-FB consumes such an
unfolded $\mathcal{X}$ and produces a disentangled
$\mathcal{U} \times \mathcal{V}$.


%%% Subsection 5.3 Constructing X via LLM text embeddings %%%
\subsection{Constructing $\mathcal{X}$ via LLM text embeddings}\label{subsec:preprocessing}

% --- Para 1: 位置づけ ---
In principle, OT-FB presumes unfolded body data in the item's own
modality---for music, an embedding learned from the sound itself. Such data
is not always at hand; for the rest, we describe a makeshift that borrows
language.

% --- Para 2: レシピ ---
Our recipe: describe each item in text and pass the description through an
LLM text embedding. A well-written description---an abstract for a paper, a
synopsis for a film---can be used as is. Where none exists, one is built from
the item's tags; songs, for example, carry tags spanning genre, mood, era,
style, and instrument, stitched into \textit{``This item has the following
    tags: $t_1$, $t_2$, \ldots, $t_N$.''}

% --- Para 3: なぜ機能するか + 一般性 ---
LLM embeddings carry far more than the words they read. \textit{Chill} and
\textit{relaxed} land near one another because the model has absorbed
countless contexts in which they appear together; whenever \textit{natural}
and \textit{acoustic} appear together, the model conjures not two independent
labels but a single sensory image---distilled from every context in which this
pairing has occurred.
Genre-ness and atmosphere arrive entangled in $\mathcal{X}$---as the unfolded
space requires, and as OT-FB will resolve.

%%% Subsection 5.4 Disentanglement on real data %%%
\subsection{Disentanglement on real data}\label{subsec:msd}

We apply OT-FB to the Million Song
Dataset~\cite{Bertin-Mahieux2011}: 2{,}500 songs evenly distributed
across ten genres, each tagged via Last.fm. Tags are passed through
\texttt{text-embedding-3-large} (OpenAI) following the recipe above, giving
the unfolded universe $\mathcal{X}$. Genre labels assign each item
to a genre in hard form; both the
genre-ness coordinate $\mathcal{U}$ and the atmosphere coordinate
$\mathcal{V}$ are learned as two-dimensional manifolds.


Figure~\ref{fig:msd_bundle}a fits each genre as its own manifold
without cross-genre alignment. The manifolds emerge, but their
internal axes point in unrelated directions: ten disentangled
representations, each with its own atmosphere coordinate, none
sharing one with another. This is the visual counterpart of
Section~\ref{subsec:OT-hypothesis}: statistical independence within
each genre is achieved, yet $v$ has no shared meaning across them.
The bias OT-FB supplies is precisely the bias that aligns these
axes; Figure~\ref{fig:msd_bundle}b shows the result.

\paragraph{What rabbit holes formed?}
Figure~\ref{fig:msd_bundle}c shows the genre-ness space $\mathcal{U}$.
The ten genres land at positions reflecting their mutual proximity as
unnamed affect: Classical stands apart; Rock and Pop cluster tightly;
Jazz, Hip-hop, Soul, and Disco collect near one another at a distance
from Classical and Country. The discrete genre label has become a
continuous coordinate---genre-ness, not genre.

Figure~\ref{fig:msd_bundle}d shows the atmosphere space $\mathcal{V}$;
plotted are the twenty-five tags most informative about $v$, selected by
mutual information with $\mathcal{V}$. They span all ten genres:
\textit{trip~hop}, \textit{downtempo}, and \textit{abstract} occupy one
region; \textit{acoustic}, \textit{melancholic}, and \textit{beautiful}
another; \textit{party}, \textit{dance}, and \textit{latin} a third.
Tags that do not cross genre boundaries---\textit{orchestral}, \textit{christmas},
\textit{japanese}---carry little information about $v$ and do not emerge. One region---acoustic,
melancholic---is the contemplative atmosphere that
Section~\ref{subsec:OT-hypothesis} anticipated; the others are atmospheres of
equal standing. What $\mathcal{V}$ has organized is not the tags themselves,
but the atmospheric character to which they point.

The two layers come apart. $\mathcal{U}$ orders genres by their unnamed
affective proximity; $\mathcal{V}$ sweeps an atmospheric character common to all
ten yet belonging to none. \emph{Common to all genres yet independent of any}:
within this dataset, the requirement we set in
Section~\ref{subsec:what-representation} is met. The internal structure of
$\mathcal{V}$ echoes Russell's circumplex model of
affect~\cite{Russell1980}---an organization recovered from data, not constructed
by hand.

The soft-genre extension applied to children's literature yields a
structure of the same character; see the Supplement.
