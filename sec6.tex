\section{Implementation: The Multi-Floor Library as Interface}\label{sec:systems}

With the representation in place, we arrive at the \textbf{fourth of the five
    levels---the Implementational}: how to let users walk in it. We did the simplest
thing possible. We rendered the multi-floor library directly as the
interface---each element of the library, one to one, as an element of the UI
(Figure~\ref{fig:prototype_systems}). Aside from this being a web
application,\footnote{Two domain instantiations of this design are accessible:
    music (\url{https://serendipity-portal.vercel.app/music/index.html})
    and children's literature
    (\url{https://serendipity-portal.vercel.app/childbooks/index.html}).} nothing special was done at the
interface level.

Because the interface \emph{is} the library, the three requirements
\textbf{(R1--R3)} of Section~\ref{subsec:multi_floor_library} pass through
unchanged. The user positions the Focus of Interest (FoI) anywhere by mouse
and browses or plays the items nearby, making the space tangible and one's
position locatable \textbf{(R1)}. Because the space separates genre-ness and
atmosphere, each direction is separable and its consequences foreseeable
\textbf{(R2)}. Around every FoI position, a rabbit-hole opening is always
shown; the user decides at will whether to plunge through it into another genre
or to move freely within the current floor, keeping the space steerable and
traversable \textbf{(R3)}. The interface inherits them at no further cost.

This is not the only interface this representation admits. The same bundle
can equally be navigated, say, by a dual-map design in which the genre-ness
space $\mathcal{U}$ and the atmosphere space $\mathcal{V}$ are presented as
independent two-dimensional maps---a four-dimensional exploration of the
bundle (Figures~\ref{fig:msd_bundle}c,d).\footnote{A four-dimensional dual-map instantiation on children's
    literature is available at
    \url{https://serendipity-portal.vercel.app/childbooks-dual/index.html}.} What matters is that, with a sound representation in place, interface
design becomes genuine craft rather than the burden of carrying
the structure. We now turn to what users do inside it.
