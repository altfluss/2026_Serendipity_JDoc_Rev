\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

This tale set out from Miles Davis and Debussy. What this paper has
sought, throughout, is what their music shares---an affinity that
listeners feel but cannot quite put into words.

We did not need to put that affinity into words. We needed to give it
a definite form in mathematics. Once that form was well-posed, the
rest---hard as it was---became a problem we could solve.

We once lived in a world where the affinity could be told only as a
paradox. Chasing it toward a well-defined form---the OT
hypothesis---we came through to a world where the tension holds no
longer. The serendipity paradox, at last, is disentangled.
